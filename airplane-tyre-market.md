<p><p>The airplane tire market is primarily divided into two categories: main tires and front tires. Main tires are designed to support the bulk of the aircraft's weight during takeoff, landing, and taxiing, providing significant durability and shock absorption. In contrast, front tires, also known as nose gear tires, are smaller and play a crucial role in steering and stability on the ground. Both types must withstand high pressures and varying weather conditions, ensuring safety and performance in aviation operations.</p></p>
