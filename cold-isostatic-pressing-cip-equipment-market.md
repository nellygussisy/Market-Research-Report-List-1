<p><p>Cold Isostatic Pressing (CIP) involves applying uniform pressure to materials to improve their density and eliminate porosity. The market is divided into two main types: dry bag pressing and wet bag pressing. Dry bag pressing utilizes a flexible bag containing the material, allowing for the application of pressure without any fluid. In contrast, wet bag pressing involves a liquid medium within the bag, which aids in achieving more consistent pressure distribution and enhancing the compaction process.</p></p>
