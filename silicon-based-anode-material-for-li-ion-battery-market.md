<p><p>Silicon-based anode materials for lithium-ion batteries primarily include silicon oxide/carbon (SiO/C) and silicon/carbon (Si/C) composites. SiO/C combines silicon oxide with carbon to enhance electrochemical performance and stability, allowing for higher capacity while mitigating volumetric expansion during cycling. Si/C composites, on the other hand, leverage pure silicon particles coated or mixed with carbon to improve conductivity and cycle life, offering a more efficient charge and discharge mechanism. Both types aim to enhance battery efficiency and longevity.</p></p>
