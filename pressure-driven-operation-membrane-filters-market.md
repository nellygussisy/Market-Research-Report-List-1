<p><p>Pressure-driven operation membrane filters encompass four main types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, nanofiltration, and reverse osmosis. Microfiltration is used for separating larger particles and suspended solids, while ultrafiltration targets smaller organic molecules and pathogens. Nanofiltration further removes divalent ions, making it suitable for softening water. Reverse osmosis provides the highest contaminant rejection, capable of desalinating seawater. Each type utilizes a membrane to separate components based on size and charge, tailoring solutions for various applications in water treatment and process industries.</p></p>
